Socrates (470-399 BCE)

An Athenian philosopher who is often credited as the founder of Western philosophy. Socrates is known for his contribution to the field of ethics and his Socratic method of argument.

Plato (428-348 BCE)

A student of Socrates and the teacher of Aristotle, Plato founded the Academy in Athens. His writings explored justice, beauty, and equality, and also the theory of forms.

Aristotle (384-322 BCE)

A student of Plato and a teacher to Alexander the Great, Aristotle wrote on various subjects, including physics, metaphysics, poetry, theater, music, logic, rhetoric, politics, and ethics.
